## Recommendations

**Top 3 service-documentation improvements**
1. **Document the invoice/reporting service boundaries** (which layer owns what across `InvoiceService`/`GenerateInvoiceBaseService`/jobs and `ReportService`/`PerformanceReportService`/`Metabase`) — the most confusing, revenue-critical area.
2. **Responsibility breakdowns for the god-services** (Thread/Business/User) — partially mitigates the departed-design-owner risk.
3. **Clarify the two Ocular dispatch jobs** (`SendOcularEvent` vs `SendToOcular`) — small, removes real ambiguity.

**Top 3 onboarding improvements**
1. Point `first-week` at the Service Catalog as the "what each service does + who owns it" reference.
2. Add the System Atlas diagram to onboarding orientation (fast mental model of external deps).
3. A "start here per domain" map: domain → canonical doc → owning service → maintainer.

**Top 3 maintainability improvements**
1. Treat the 6 oversized services as **refactor candidates** (extract responsibilities) — reduces bus-factor blast radius.
2. Resolve the overlapping-responsibility boundaries (clear ownership per layer).
3. Keep the catalog refreshed alongside the ownership map (both git-derivable).

**What I would document next:** **service-boundary clarification for the invoice/reporting cluster** — it's the highest-confusion, highest-revenue area, fully repo-derivable, and directly supports incident response on the money path.

**What documentation work now has low ROI and should stop:** net-new **narrative/explanatory prose** and any further **top-level index/atlas creation** — the structural layer (domains, data-model, ownership, service catalog, risk register) is now complete; additional breadth has diminishing returns. Depth on the few confusing boundaries + external validation of parked findings is where value remains.
