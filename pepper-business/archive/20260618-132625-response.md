## Recommendations

**Top 3 ownership improvements**
1. **Assign distinct 2nd reviewers** for the orphan-risk areas (Identity/Auth, Metabase, VAT) — the single most effective way to break the 2-person bus factor. Encoded as TODOs in `CODEOWNERS.recommended`.
2. **Activate CODEOWNERS** (confirm GitLab handles → move to `.gitlab/CODEOWNERS`) so reviews are routed and ownership is enforced, not implicit.
3. **Capture design knowledge for Threads & Identity** (departed-owner areas) — a short "how this subsystem works / gotchas" note from the current maintainers.

**Top 3 onboarding improvements**
1. Link the ownership map into onboarding `first-week` ("who to ask per domain").
2. A **service → external-system dependency map** (pairs with ownership for incident scoping).
3. Glossary/orientation note: the 3 active maintainers and their primary domains.

**Top 3 maintainability improvements**
1. CODEOWNERS activation + 2nd-reviewer assignment (redundancy).
2. Quarterly refresh of the ownership map (commands embedded).
3. Pair-up rotation on the thinnest areas (Metabase, VAT) to spread knowledge.

**What I would document next:** a **service → external-system dependency map** (services ↔ Metabase/GAM/Pepper B2C/networks/accounting). It complements ownership (who + what-it-talks-to = fast incident scoping), is fully repo-derivable from `app/Services/*` + config, and rounds out the onboarding/operational picture.

**What documentation work now has low ROI and should stop:** net-new **narrative/explanatory prose** (more domain essays, onboarding sub-guides, atlas expansions) — the corpus is mature and code-strong; and re-deriving the **parked findings** (M1/L1/GAM) — they're preserved; only external validation adds value now.
